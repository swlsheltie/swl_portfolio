ABSTRACT

patterns of abstraction is a multi-piece poster series based on the process of machine learning.

overview

Inspired by the work of Luna Maurer, known for her development of field of conditional design, I wanted to create something in the flavor of group participatory design that went beyond arbitrary rules or "conditions". Instead, I was more fascinated by the rules and the process of creating itself, than the end result (though I also had a somewhat specific image/result in mind when creating the rules behind the posters).

These posters were inspired by the process of image recognition and neural nets. I broke down the computational steps behind a simplified image recognition process, and had a human participant take the role of each of those 6-7 steps. Starting from a "blank" poster with hundreds of randomized images, I had each person in the workflow pass on what they interpreted from the images to the nextâ€”the last person, using the compounded abstractions from each "layer" before them, would attempt to name a specific memory or experience that the final image reminded them of.

Through this process, I wanted to explore how human recognition still differs from computer recognition, and maybe, how they might converge in the future.
